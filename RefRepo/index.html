Week 1

Questions

  • What makes an experience spiritual?
  • How do we make experiences a scalable product?
    • Design systems
    • Crowdsourcing
    • AR? Accessible at any point with the ubiquity of devices
  • How do we build experiences without excessive use of resources in an environmentally sustainable way?
    • Have people leave their screens and engage more with each other?
    • Sound - voice + audio replacing screens in the future?
  • Under what circumstances is design a necessity and not supplementary?
  • How do we change the cultural conversation and perception around education?
  • How can we financially support artists and creators without the distortion caused by corporate sponsorship? What kind of monetization structure could sustain creative endeavors in a way that leaves creators unfettered?

Thoughts + Observations

  • Poster project: Art Hill, space where man and nature meet
    • How the open air interacts with the rising morning sun
    • Space of community and meditation
    • Transcendentalism, the Hudson River School
  • Natural order, organicism, fibonacci sequence --> Frank Lloyd Wright, unique module-based systems
    • Geometric in form, organic in rhythm
  • Performance: cyclical motion of energy between performer and audience, feeling of unity, uniform experience imparted despite each person's different perspective
  • Globalism: studying other cultures and seeing what hey say about the human experience
  • Design as journalism and investigation of truth

Readings

  • A Lesson from Frank Lloyd Wright: Patterns of Nature
    • "Let us learn to see within, at least far enough to grasp essential pattern in all created things. Method in creation will come freely to him who learns to see in the abstract. Study the geometry that is the idea of every form: a quail, a snail, a shell, a fish. Take for analysis the more simple, obvious things first."
  • Computational Organicism: Examining Evolutionary Design Strategies in Architecture
    • "Today the debasement of geological forms as “unorganized” is clearly disputed by current scientific theories of self-organization and emergence, which ascribe to matter the inherent generative capacity for spontaneous development of complex order."
